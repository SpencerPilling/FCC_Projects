A Brief history about Duke Nukem 3D

Duke Nukem 3D was created in 1996 by 3Drealms. This was the third chapter of the Duke Nukem franchise, and the first of the chapters to bring a 3D perspective to the game. The first two Duke Nukem's were side scrollers. In Duke Nukem 3D, Duke returns to earth to find aliens have taken over again. Landing on top a building in the destruction of Los Angeles, Duke must start on his journey to rid the earth of another alien race.

Episode one "LA Meltdown"

In this first episode, Duke battles his way through LA to discover that the aliens have been taking the women of LA. When he finds the alien ship, he destroys it, in good ol' Duke Nukem fashion. This episode includes a strip club, red light district and a prison.

Episode two "Lunar Apocalypse"

In the second episode, Duke travels up to space to see what the aliens were doing with all the women they had captured. It turns out the aliens were being held in incubators, when Duke finally gets to the mother ship on the moon, he destroys the alien overlord. Duke then finds out that this was all a distraction, the aliens have started to attack earth in his absence.

Episode three "Shrapnel City"

in the third episode, Duke travels back to earth to take on the aliens in LA for another round. He is able to find the leader of the alien race, the Cycloid Emperor, and ends its life. When the game comes to a end, Duke Nukem promises after some R&R, he will be ready for more action.

Bonus Episodes

There are two bonus episodes that were released after the initial game. The fourth episode was called the Atomic Edition Episode Four, The Birth. Then in October of 2016, Duke Nukem was back for Episode Five, Alien World Order, in the release of Duke Nukem 3D: 20th Anniversary World Tour
